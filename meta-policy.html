Meta / Facebook Data Policy

Applies to: CarVerge | Last updated: May 1, 2025
Overview

CarVerge, operated by James Carr, uses Meta (Facebook) advertising tools to measure the performance of content and advertising campaigns. This page explains specifically how Meta's tools are deployed on this site, what data is collected, how it is used, and what your options are.

Meta's tools - primarily the Meta Pixel and the Conversions API - are advertising measurement technologies operated by Meta Platforms, Inc. (Menlo Park, California). This page supplements our Privacy Policy and provides Meta-specific transparency required by Meta's advertising policies and applicable privacy law.

What the Meta Pixel Does

The Meta Pixel is a small piece of JavaScript code installed on this website. When you visit a page on CarVerge, the Pixel fires and sends certain information to Meta. Specifically, the Pixel collects:

  • The URL of the page you visited
  • Your IP address (which Meta may use to match you to a Facebook or Instagram account)
  • Browser type and operating system
  • A unique browser identifier stored in a cookie
  • Standard event data - for example, whether you viewed a specific article, scrolled to the bottom of a page, or clicked a newsletter signup button

We use this data to understand which content drives engagement, to measure the effectiveness of any paid advertising we run on Facebook or Instagram, and to build Custom Audiences of people who have shown interest in EV & Automotive content so we can reach similar audiences.

Custom Audiences

Based on Meta Pixel data, we may create Custom Audiences in our Meta Ads account. These audiences allow us to show advertising on Facebook and Instagram to people who have previously visited this site. For example, we might show newsletter sign-up ads to people who read multiple articles but did not subscribe.

We may also create Lookalike Audiences - groups of people on Meta's platforms who share characteristics with our existing audience. Lookalike Audiences are built by Meta using its own matching algorithms; we do not have access to the individual data Meta uses to construct them.

We do not use Custom Audiences created from CarVerge data to run advertising on behalf of third parties. These audiences are used exclusively for CarVerge promotional purposes.

Data Sharing with Meta

Through the Meta Pixel, the following categories of data may be shared with Meta Platforms, Inc.:

  • Browsing behavior on this site (pages viewed, time on site, interactions)
  • IP addresses (used by Meta for account matching)
  • Cookie identifiers that may link your visit to a Meta account
  • Email address hashed (if you sign up for our newsletter and we activate the advanced matching feature)

Meta uses this data in accordance with its own Data Policy, available at facebook.com/privacy/policy. Meta may combine data received from our Pixel with data from other sources to build advertising profiles. We do not control how Meta processes data received from our Pixel beyond what is configured in our Meta Ads Manager settings.

Meta's Use of Your Data

Meta may use data collected through our Pixel for its own purposes, including improving ad targeting across its platforms, building advertising models, and personalizing content shown to you on Facebook and Instagram. Meta's use of this data is governed by Meta's Data Policy, not by this policy.

If you have a Facebook or Instagram account, Meta may associate your visit to CarVerge with your account based on your IP address, browser cookies, or login state.

Your Options and Opt-Out

You have several options for controlling the Meta Pixel's collection of data from your visits to this site:

  • Facebook Ad Preferences: Visit facebook.com/ads/preferences to review and adjust your ad preferences, including interest-based advertising settings.
  • Off-Facebook Activity: Visit facebook.com/off-facebook-activity to review and disconnect activity that businesses and organizations have shared with Meta about you, including visits to this site.
  • Browser settings: Blocking third-party cookies in your browser will prevent the Meta Pixel from setting cookies on your device. Note that this may not prevent all Pixel data collection.
  • Browser extensions: Ad blockers and privacy-focused browser extensions (such as uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger) typically block the Meta Pixel.
  • Do Not Track: This site honors browser Do Not Track signals to the extent technically possible, but Meta's own processing of data is governed by their policies.
Data Retention

Custom Audiences created from CarVerge Pixel data are retained in our Meta Ads account for as long as we operate advertising campaigns. We delete unused audiences periodically. Meta retains the underlying event data it collects through the Pixel according to its own data retention policies.

Legal Basis for Processing

For visitors in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, our use of the Meta Pixel is based on our legitimate interest in measuring content performance and advertising effectiveness. You have the right to object to this processing. To exercise this right, use the opt-out options described above or contact us directly.

Contact

For questions about our use of Meta advertising tools, contact James Carr at CarVerge. For questions about Meta's own data practices, visit Meta's Help Center at facebook.com/help or contact Meta directly at facebook.com/privacy.
