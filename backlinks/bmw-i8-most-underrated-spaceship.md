It's a performance car that happens to be efficient. That's the wrong frame—it's an efficient drivetrain in a performance package. The cognitive distance between those two framings mattered.

That scissor-door design, the proportions, the carbon fiber visibility—it looked like it didn't belong on regular roads. People found that unsettling rather than compelling. Now? Every luxury EV borrows liberally from that exact design language.
